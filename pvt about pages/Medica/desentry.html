About Implants

A dental implant is an artificial tooth root that a periodontics places into your jaw to hold a replacement tooth or bridge. Dental implants are ideal for people who are in good general oral health but have lost teeth due to periodontal disease, an injury, or some other reason. Although high-tech in nature, dental implants are actually more tooth-saving than traditional bridgework since implants do not rely on the neighboring teeth for support.

Dental implants look and feel so natural that you may totally forget that you ever lost a tooth or two. You know that your confidence about your teeth affects how you feel about yourself both personally and professionally. Perhaps you hide your smile because of the spaces from missing teeth. Maybe your dentures don’t feel secure. Possibly you cannot eat properly due to difficulties in chewing. If you are missing one or more teeth and would like to smile, speak, and eat again with comfort and confidence, there is a solution – dental implants.

Under proper conditions, such as placement by a periodontist and diligent patient maintenance, implants can last a lifetime. Long-term studies continue to show improving success rates for dental implants.

How Can Dental Implants Help

Dental implants are primarily used to achieve the following:

  • Replace one or more teeth without affecting bordering teeth
  • Support a bridge and eliminate the need for a removable partial denture
  • Provide support for a denture, making it more secure and comfortable
